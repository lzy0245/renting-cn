# Rental Decision Framework

Use this reference when comparing areas, screening listings, combining Redbook evidence with other sources, or producing a recommendation.

## Source Triangulation

Do not let one source dominate the decision. Use each source for what it is good at:

| Source | Good For | Weak For |
|---|---|---|
| Xiaohongshu/Redbook | lived experience, recurring complaints, neighborhood feel, agency or brand reputation | ownership, availability, payment safety, exact rent truth |
| Listing/platform/user screenshots | layout, rent claim, photos, fees, availability claim | hidden defects, bait listings, authority |
| Maps/public web | commute, nearby facilities, official policies, broad market context | exact unit condition and contract safety |
| On-site viewing | actual condition, noise, smell, light, defects, building access | signer authority and legal terms |
| Documents/contract | signer, payment, deposit, repair, early termination, sublet consent | lived experience and hidden environmental issues |

## Decision Dimensions

Evaluate only dimensions relevant to the user's task:

- Fit: commute, budget, location, room type, lease term, lifestyle constraints, move-in time.
- Total cost: rent, deposit, payment cycle, agency fee, service fee, utilities, internet, property, cleaning, parking, hidden mandatory charges.
- Market credibility: comparable rent, reason for low price, stale listing risk, promotional conditions.
- Living condition: lighting, ventilation, noise, dampness, pests, safety, appliances, water, electricity, gas, internet, elevator, corridor, fire exits.
- Channel and authority: poster role, signer, owner/authorization proof, sublet permission, payee consistency.
- Contract and payment: deposit refund, repair duty, early termination, renewal, handover, verbal promises, receipts.
- Social-platform evidence: Redbook note quality, comments, repeated warnings, author credibility, recency.

## Recommendation Thresholds

Use these tendencies:

- 推荐看房: key facts are plausible, total cost is within range, no high-risk authority/payment signal, and Redbook signals are neutral or manageable.
- 谨慎推进: medium risks exist but can be checked before viewing/signing.
- 先追问再看: essential facts are missing, especially total cost, signer, availability, exact unit, or fees.
- 暂不建议: high-risk payment, authority, safety, illegal partition, severe repeated complaints, or refusal to verify.
- 信息不足: facts are too sparse; provide targeted collection steps.

## Market Credibility

Low rent is not automatically bad, but it needs a reason:

- Old building, no elevator, lower floor, long walk to metro, short remaining lease, tenant transfer urgency, defects, shared or partitioned space, poor light, noise, or strict payment cycle.

Treat these as high risk:

- Far-below-market rent plus immediate payment pressure.
- Total cost not disclosed.
- Signer/payee/poster are different with no written explanation.
- Photos avoid windows, bathroom, kitchen, entrance, corridor, and exterior.
- Listing cannot provide a current walkthrough video for the exact unit.

## Handling Conflicts

If sources conflict, rank evidence by verifiability:

1. Current official/source documents and signed written terms for legal or policy facts.
2. On-site inspection and current exact-unit video for actual condition.
3. Repeated independent renter reports for lived experience.
4. Listing claims and agent statements.
5. Engagement metrics and vague social comments.

Explain the conflict and the exact check that resolves it.
