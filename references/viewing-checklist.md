# Viewing Checklist

Use this reference for viewing preparation, on-site inspection, evidence collection, and no-pay conditions.

## Before Viewing

Confirm before spending travel time:

- Is this the exact unit from the photos/video, and is it currently available?
- Earliest move-in date, lease term, and whether short lease or transfer is involved.
- Total monthly and one-time cost: rent, deposit, payment cycle, agency fee, service fee, property, utilities, internet, cleaning, parking, and other mandatory charges.
- Who signs the lease and who receives deposit/rent.
- Whether ownership, authorization, or sublet consent can be verified before signing.
- Whether viewing or holding the unit requires any fee. Treat required pre-viewing payment as high risk.

## On-Site Checks

Inspect and test:

- Door locks, peephole, access control, windows, screens, curtains, and security.
- Water pressure, drainage, toilet, shower, water heater, kitchen faucet, gas, range hood, stove, and leakage.
- Air conditioner, heating, lights, sockets, major appliances, refrigerator, washing machine, and internet installation options.
- Walls, ceiling, corners, cabinets, bathroom, balcony, window frames, and exterior walls for dampness, mold, cracks, odor, fresh paint, or pests.
- Lighting, orientation, ventilation, window view, privacy, and whether there are blocked windows or converted spaces.
- Noise with windows open and closed: road, rail, school, restaurant, elevator, neighbors, construction, square dancing, delivery traffic.
- Corridor, elevator, fire exits, stairs, trash area, parcel area, cameras, bike/e-bike charging, and emergency access.

## Evidence To Record

Before signing or paying:

- Full walkthrough video from building entrance to the unit, if possible.
- Photos/videos of defects, appliances, furniture, meter readings, keys, access cards, and existing damage.
- Chat records for promised repairs, included furniture, fees, payment cycle, and move-in date.
- Written handover list with item condition and meter readings.

## Redbook-Informed Viewing

Convert Redbook signals into targeted checks:

- Noise complaints -> visit during commute peak, evening, or weekend if the complaint mentions timing.
- Dampness/mold -> inspect bathroom, kitchen, window frames, outer walls, wardrobes, and recently painted areas.
- Deposit disputes -> ask for written refund timeline, deduction standard, cleaning standard, and handover process.
- Bad property management -> inspect elevator, parcels, security, fire exits, trash, repairs, and resident notices.
- Agency complaints -> ask for fee schedule, exact signer, authorization, and whether commitments enter the contract.

## Do Not Pay Yet If

- You have not seen the exact unit in person or through a live current video.
- The signer, owner/authorization holder, poster, and payee relationship is unclear.
- The other party refuses ownership/authorization/sublet-consent verification.
- Total cost, deposit refund, or early termination rules are vague.
- The unit appears partitioned, unsafe, overcrowded, or inconsistent with the listing.
- You are pressured to pay immediately because of "many people waiting".
- Important promises remain only verbal or only in disappearing messages.
